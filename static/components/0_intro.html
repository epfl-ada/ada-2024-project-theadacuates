Main Question:
Is Cognitive Fatigue Quantifiable in Wikispeedia, and What Role Does It Play in Gameplay?

Recent studies show that our attention spans are shrinking, and our brains can become fatigued within minutes of focused activity (1). This project aims to investigate user engagement with the Wikispeedia game from a neuroscientific angle, focusing on how article complexity impacts cognitive load and behavior. Key questions include:

  • How does the complexity of articles affect user engagement?
  • Does the structure and layout (e.g., headings, multimedia) influence time spent, back-clicks, or path abandonment?
  • Are there biological biases that influence the gameplay?

The dataset used in the analysis can be found in here and includes the paths of approximately 75,000 games, along with ancillary information such as the game’s rating and the number of backlinks. Additionally, each path could either be completed or unfinished, depending on whether the user completes the mission. This distinction is particularly important, as unfinished paths will be used as proxies for mental fatigue.

To shed light on this intricate question, we will delve into a discussion between the diligent student Pepito and his mentor, Mr. Cajal. Pepito is a young neuroscience student with big dreams and... questionable study habits. Last week, he faced an unexpected academic crisis—not in the lab, but in a game called Wikispeedia. The result? Pepito got lost. A lot. Like, "How did I end up reading about Cabbage Moths?" lost. Embarrassed but determined, Pepito boldly blamed the game, not himself. "It’s not me—it’s cognitive overload! This game would exhaust even the sharpest minds." But Pepito's mentor, the legendary Dr. Ramón y Cajal, wasn’t buying it. Known for spotting an excuse from a mile away, Dr. Cajal challenged Pepito to prove his claims with data. What followed was a heated back-and-forth between Pepito and Dr. Cajal. Could Pepito’s data reveal the game’s inherent challenge, or would Dr. Cajal’s “study harder” mantra prevail?
