Before proceeding with Pepito's findings, let's start with a brief explanation and example of what semantic similarity is.

Semantic similarity refers to the degree to which two concepts, words, or phrases are related in meaning, even if they are not identical. In essence, it allows us to evaluate how closely words or sentences are associated based on their meanings rather than exact matches.
